"As I often say, we have come a long way from the days of slavery, but in 2014, discrimination and inequality still saturate our society in modern ways. Though racism may be less blatant now in many cases, its existence is undeniable." - Al Sharpton The events recently here in the United States regarding OSU has brought this image back to my mind. Once again, in this self proclaimed great nation, we find ourselves in a racially charged controversy and I feel the quote above is very poignant. I finally got to see the video and it's things like this that make me feel unsafe to travel the country. And despite any argument that I'm sure will be thrown out stating my concerns are not valid, you are most certainly wrong. And despite not living in the fifties and sixties when a lot of this was presented directly in the face of minorities, I have the experience to prove otherwise. It disturbs me to hear things like this. To hear people promoting such hate and ignorance simply because somebody is different. And of course the door swings both ways as I am opposed to reverse racism equally. But I feel as though when it comes down to the issue of racism in the United States, I don't feel as though enough children from that older generation are being taught to dismiss hateful speech. I feel it's simply being covered up and viewed as something that the individual is only chastised for being caught saying it.
